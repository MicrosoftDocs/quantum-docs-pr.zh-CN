# <a name="understanding-quantum-computing"></a><span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-103">了解量子计算</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-103">Understanding quantum computing</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-104">量子计算运用量子力学原理来处理信息。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-104">Quantum computing uses the principles of quantum mechanics to process information.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-105">因此，量子计算需要的方法不同于经典计算。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-105">Because of this, quantum computing requires a different approach than classical computing.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-106">这种差异的一个例子是量子计算机中使用的处理器。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-106">One example of this difference is the processor used in quantum computers.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-107">经典计算机使用熟悉的硅材料芯片，而量子计算机使用原子、离子、光子或电子等量子系统。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-107">Where classical computers use familiar silicon-based chips, quantum computers use quantum systems such as atoms, ions, photons, or electrons.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-108">它们使用其量子属性来表示可以在 1 和 0 的不同量子叠加中准备的位。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-108">They use their quantum properties to represent bits that can be prepared in different quantum superpositions of 1 and 0.</span></span>  

<span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-109">量子材料的行为符合量子力学定律，并且利用概率计算、叠加和纠缠等概念。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-109">The quantum material behaves according to the laws of quantum mechanics, leveraging concepts such as probabilistic computation, superposition, and entanglement.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-110">这些概念为利用量子计算解决复杂问题的量子算法提供了基础。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-110">These concepts provide the basis for quantum algorithms that harness the power of quantum computing to solve complex problems.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-111">本文介绍了量子计算所基于的一些量子力学基本概念。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-111">This article describes some of the essential concepts of quantum mechanics on which quantum computing is based.</span></span>

## <a name="a-birds-eye-view-of-quantum-mechanics"></a><span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-112">量子力学鸟瞰图</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-112">A bird’s-eye view of quantum mechanics</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-113">量子力学（也称为量子理论）是物理学的一个分支，它处理原子和亚原子级的粒子。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-113">Quantum mechanics, also called quantum theory, is a branch of physics that deals with particles at the atomic and subatomic levels.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-114">但是，在量子层面上，你所认为的许多力学定律都不适用。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-114">At the quantum level, however, many of the laws of mechanics you take for granted don’t apply.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-115">叠加、量子测量和纠缠是量子计算的三大核心现象。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-115">Superposition, quantum measurement, and entanglement are three phenomena that are central to quantum computing.</span></span>  

### <a name="superposition-vs-binary-computing"></a><span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-116">叠加与二进制计算</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-116">Superposition vs. binary computing</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-117">假设你在客厅锻炼身体。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-117">Imagine that you are exercising in your living room.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-118">你一直向左转，然后一直向右转。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-118">You turn all the way to your left and then all the way to your right.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-119">现在，同时向左转和向右转。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-119">Now turn to your left and your right at the same time.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-120">你无法做到这一点（至少要将自己一分为二）。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-120">You can’t do it (not without splitting yourself in two, at least).</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-121">显然，你无法同时处于这两种状态，即无法同时向左和向右。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-121">Obviously, you can’t be in both of those states at once – you can’t be facing left and facing right at the same time.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-122">但如果你是量子粒子，由于一种称为叠加（也称为相干性）的现象，你可能具有一定的向左的概率和一定的向右的概率  。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-122">However, if you are a quantum particle, then you can have a certain probability of *facing left* AND a certain probability of *facing right* due to a phenomenon known as **superposition** (also known as **coherence**).</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-123">量子粒子（例如电子）具有其自己的“向左或向右”属性（例如被称为向上或向下的自旋），或者使其与经典二进制计算更相关，比如说 1 或 0。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-123">A quantum particle such as an electron has its own “facing left or facing right” properties, for example **spin**, referred to as either up or down, or to make it more relatable to classical binary computing, let’s just say 1 or 0.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-124">当量子粒子处于叠加状态时，它是介于 1 和 0 之间的无数个状态的线性组合，但只有在你实际观察它时，你才知道它的状态，这又会涉及到我们要讲的下一种现象，即量子测量。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-124">When a quantum particle is in a superposition state, it’s a linear combination of an infinite number of states between 1 and 0, but you don’t know which one it will be until you actually look at it, which brings up our next phenomenon, **quantum measurement**.</span></span>

### <a name="quantum-measurement"></a><span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-125">量子测量</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-125">Quantum measurement</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-126">现在，假设你的朋友来了，并且想拍下你锻炼身体的照片。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-126">Now, let’s say your friend comes over and wants to take a picture of you exercising.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-127">最有可能的是，他们拍下的照片是介于你一直向左转和一直向右转之间的某个位置的模糊图像。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-127">Most likely, they’ll get a blurry image of you turning somewhere between all the way left and all the way right.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-128">但是，如果你是量子粒子，就会发生一件有趣的事情。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-128">But if you’re a quantum particle, an interesting thing happens.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-129">当他们拍照时无论你在哪个位置，照片都将始终显示向左转或向右转，而不会介于两者之间。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-129">No matter where you are when they take the picture, it will always show you either all the way left or all the way right – nothing in-between.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-130">这是因为观察或测量量子粒子会使叠加状态（也称为退相干）坍缩，并且粒子呈现 1 或 0 的经典二元状态 。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-130">This is because the act of observing or measuring a quantum particle **collapses** the superposition state (also known as **decoherence**) and the particle takes on a classical binary state of either 1 or 0.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-131">这种二进制状态对我们很有帮助，因为在计算中，你可以在 1 和 0 的状态下执行许多操作。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-131">This binary state is helpful to us, because in computing you can do lots of things with 1’s and 0’s.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-132">但是，在量子粒子经过测量和坍缩后，它将永远保持该状态（就像你的照片一样），并且始终为 1 或 0。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-132">However, once a quantum particle has been measured and collapsed, it stays in that state forever (just like your picture) and will always be a 1 or 0.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-133">不过，正如你稍后将看到的那样，在量子计算中，有些操作可以将粒子“重置”为叠加状态，因此可以再次使用它进行量子计算。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-133">As you’ll see later, though, in quantum computing there are operations that can “reset” a particle back to a superposition state so it can be used for quantum calculations again.</span></span>

### <a name="entanglement"></a><span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-134">纠缠</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-134">Entanglement</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-135">量子力学中最有趣的现象可能是两个或多个量子粒子彼此纠缠。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-135">Possibly the most interesting phenomenon of quantum mechanics is the ability of two or more quantum particles to become **entangled** with each other.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-136">当粒子纠缠在一起时，它们会形成单个系统，因此不能独立于其他粒子的量子状态来描述任何一个粒子的量子状态。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-136">When particles become entangled, they form a single system such that the quantum state of any one particle cannot be described independently of the quantum state of the other particles.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-137">这意味着，对一个粒子应用的任何操作或过程也会与其他粒子相关。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-137">This means that whatever operation or process you apply to one particle correlates to the other particles as well.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-138">除了这种相互依赖性，粒子甚至还可以在相距甚远的距离（甚至是光年）也可以保持这种连接。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-138">In addition to this interdependency, particles can maintain this connection even when separated over incredibly large distances, even light-years.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-139">量子测量的效果也适用于纠缠的粒子，因此当一个粒子经过测量和坍缩后，其他粒子也将坍缩。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-139">The effects of quantum measurement also apply to entangled particles, such that when one particle is measured and collapses, the other particle collapses as well.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-140">由于纠缠的量子比特之间存在相干性，因此测量一个量子比特的状态可以提供有关其他量子比特的状态的信息，这种特殊的属性在量子计算中非常有用。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-140">Because there is a correlation between the entangled qubits, measuring the state of one qubit provides information about the state of the other qubit – this particular property is very helpful in quantum computing.</span></span>

### <a name="qubits-and-probability"></a><span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-141">量子比特和概率</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-141">Qubits and probability</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-142">经典计算机以比特为单位存储和处理信息，比特的状态可以为 1 或 0，但不能同时处于这两种状态。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-142">Classical computers store and process information in bits, which can have a state of either 1 or 0, but never both.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-143">量子计算中的等效项是量子比特，它表示量子粒子的状态。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-143">The equivalent in quantum computing is the **qubit**, which represents the state of a quantum particle.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-144">由于叠加，量子比特可以是 1 或 0，也可以介于这两者之间。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-144">Because of superposition, qubits can either be 1 or 0 or anything in between.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-145">根据其配置，量子比特具有坍缩为 1 或 0 的一定概率。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-145">Depending on its configuration, a qubit has a certain *probability* of collapsing to 1 or 0.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-146">量子比特坍缩方式的概率取决于量子干扰。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-146">The qubit's probability of collapsing one way or the other is determined by **quantum interference**.</span></span> 

<span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-147">还记得为你拍过照的朋友吗？</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-147">Remember your friend that was taking your picture?</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-148">假设相机上有特殊的滤镜，称为干扰滤镜。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-148">Suppose they have special filters on their camera called *Interference* filters.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-149">如果他们选择 70/30 滤镜并开始拍照，则其中 70% 的照片上的人将向左，而 30% 的照片上的人将向右。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-149">If they select the *70/30* filter and start taking pictures, in 70% of them you will be facing left, and in 30% you will be facing right.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-150">滤镜干扰了相机的正常状态，从而影响了其行为的概率。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-150">The filter has interfered with the regular state of the camera to influence the probability of its behavior.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-151">同样，量子干扰会影响量子比特的状态，从而在测量过程中影响特定结果的概率，而这种概率状态正是量子计算的优势。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-151">Similarly, quantum interference affects the state of a qubit in order to influence the probability of a certain outcome during measurement, and this probabilistic state is where the power of quantum computing excels.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-152">例如，经典计算机中只有两个比特，其中每个比特都可以存储 1 或 0，因此，你可以同时存储四个可能的值（00、01、10 和 11），但一次只能存储一个值   。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-152">For example, with two bits in a classical computer, each bit can store 1 or 0, so together you can store four possible values – **00**, **01**, **10**, and **11** – but only one of those at a time.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-153">但在两个量子比特重叠的情况下，每个量子比特都可以是 1 和/或 0，因此你可以同时表示四个相同值。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-153">With two qubits in superposition, however, each qubit can be 1 or 0 or *both*, so you can represent the same four values simultaneously.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-154">如果是三个量子比特，则可表示八个值；如果是四个量子比特，可表示 16 个值，依此类推。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-154">With three qubits, you can represent eight values, with four qubits, you can represent 16 values, and so on.</span></span>

## <a name="summary"></a><span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-155">总结</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-155">Summary</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-156">这些概念仅仅是触及了量子力学的表面，但是对于量子计算而言，这些概念具有根本的重要意义。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-156">These concepts just scratch the surface of quantum mechanics, but are fundamentally important concepts to know for quantum computing.</span></span>

- <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-157">**叠加** - 量子粒子组合为所有可能的状态的能力。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-157">**Superposition** - The ability of quantum particles to be a combination of all possible states.</span></span>
- <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-158">**量子测量** - 一种观察叠加状态下的量子粒子并生成其中某种可能的状态的行为。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-158">**Quantum measurement** - The act of observing a quantum particle in superposition and resulting in one of the possible states.</span></span>
- <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-159">**纠缠** - 量子粒子将其测量结果相互关联的能力。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-159">**Entanglement** - The ability of quantum particles to correlate their measurement results with each other.</span></span>
- <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-160">**量子比特** - 量子计算中的基本信息单位。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-160">**Qubit** - The basic unit of information in quantum computing.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-161">一个量子比特表示所有可能的状态叠加中的一个量子粒子。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-161">A qubit represents a quantum particle in superposition of all possible states.</span></span>
- <span data-ttu-id="d3e7e-162">**干扰** - 量子比特固有的行为，由于叠加而影响其坍缩方式的可能性。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="d3e7e-162">**Interference** - Intrinsic behavior of a qubit due to superposition to influence the probability of it collapsing one way or another.</span></span>
