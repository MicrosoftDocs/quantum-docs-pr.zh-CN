# <a name="what-is-quantum-computing"></a><span data-ttu-id="85548-103">什么是量子计算？</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-103">What is quantum computing?</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="85548-104">有些问题非常复杂、困难，即使世界上的所有超级计算机都工作，也要花费比宇宙寿命更长的时间来解决。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-104">There are some problems so difficult, so incredibly vast, that even if every supercomputer in the world worked on the problem, it would still take longer than the lifetime of the universe to solve.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="85548-105">量子计算机有望解决我们这个星球上一些最大的挑战 - 环境、农业、医疗保健、能源、气候、材料科学，甚至包括我们还没有想到的问题。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-105">Quantum computers hold the promise to solve some of our planet's biggest challenges - in environment, agriculture, health, energy, climate, materials science, and problems we’ve not yet even imagined.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="85548-106">量子计算机的影响将是深远的，其影响与 1947 年晶体管的诞生一样大，而晶体管的诞生为当今的数字经济铺平了道路。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-106">The impact of quantum computers will be far-reaching and have as great an impact as the creation of the transistor in 1947, which paved the way for today’s digital economy.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="85548-107">量子计算利用量子物理学的独特行为，提供了一个功能强大的全新计算模型。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-107">Quantum computing harnesses the unique behavior of quantum physics to provide a new and powerful model of computing.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="85548-108">量子物理学理论认为，在量子水平上，物质可以处于多个经典状态的叠加中。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-108">The theory of quantum physics posits that matter, at a quantum level can be in a superposition of multiple classical states.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="85548-109">许多状态相互干扰，就像潮汐池中的波浪。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-109">And those many states interfere with each other like waves in a tide pool.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="85548-110">物质的状态在度量后会“塌缩”成经典状态之一。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-110">The state of matter after a measurement "collapses" into one of the classical states.</span></span> 

<span data-ttu-id="85548-111">随后，重复进行相同的度量会生成相同的经典结果。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-111">Thereafter, repeating the same measurement will produce the same classical result.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="85548-112">如果粒子以特定方式进行交互，每个粒子的量子状态都不能在独立于其他粒子的情况下进行描述（即使这些粒子在物理上相隔很远），这种现象就叫量子纠缠。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-112">Quantum entanglement occurs when particles interact in ways such that the quantum state of each cannot be described independently of the others, even if the particles are physically far apart.</span></span>  

<span data-ttu-id="85548-113">量子计算将信息存储在物质的量子状态中，使用其量子特性（叠加和纠缠）来实现量子操作，根据该信息进行计算，用户只需利用和学习量子干涉编程即可。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-113">Quantum computing stores information in quantum states of matter and uses its quantum nature of superposition and entanglement to realize quantum operations that compute on that information, thereby harnessing and learning to program quantum interference.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="85548-114">量子计算听起来可能令人望而生畏，但有了合适的资源，现在便可以开始生成量子应用。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-114">Quantum computing might sound daunting, but with the right resources you can start building quantum applications today.</span></span>

## <a name="the-qubit"></a><span data-ttu-id="85548-115">量子位</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-115">The qubit</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="85548-116">量子计算定义那些反映量子行为的计算概念。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-116">Quantum computing defines computing concepts that reflect the quantum behavior.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="85548-117">量子计算最基础的概念是量子位。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-117">Quantum computing begins with the notion of a qubit.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="85548-118">在量子计算中，量子位表示一个量子信息单元，与经典位相同  。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-118">In quantum computing, a quantum bit - **qubit** - is a unit of quantum information, like a classical bit.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="85548-119">经典位保存单个二进制值（如 0 或 1），而量子位的状态则可以是 0 和 1 的同时**叠加**。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-119">Where classical bits hold a single binary value such as a 0 or 1, the state of a qubit can be in a **superposition** of 0 and 1 simultaneously.</span></span>  

<span data-ttu-id="85548-120">对量子位进行度量这个行为本身会改变量子位状态。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-120">The act of measuring a qubit changes a qubit state.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="85548-121">进行度量时，量子位会从叠加态变为经典状态之一。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-121">With measurement, the qubit goes from being in superposition to one of the classical states.</span></span>  

<span data-ttu-id="85548-122">多个量子位也可以**纠缠**在一起。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-122">Multiple qubits can also be **entangled**.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="85548-123">度量一个纠缠的量子位时，就会知道另一个量子位的状态。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-123">When we make a measurement of one entangled qubit, our knowledge of the state of the other(s) is updated as well.</span></span>

## <a name="quantum-algorithms"></a><span data-ttu-id="85548-124">量子算法</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-124">Quantum algorithms</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="85548-125">量子算法旨在利用量子的特性和行为来加快经典算法的速度，或者提供全新的物理系统建模方法。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-125">Quantum algorithms are designed to take advantage of quantum nature and behavior to speed up classical algorithms, or to provide entirely new ways of modeling physical systems.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="85548-126">这些算法利用了量子位的信息编码方式以及对多个处于叠加态的纠缠量子位进行的操作的并行特性。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-126">These algorithms exploit the way qubits encode information and the parallel nature of operating on multiple entangled qubits in superposition.</span></span>  

<span data-ttu-id="85548-127">经典计算机以位的形式编码信息；每个位编码两个可能的值：0 或 1。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-127">Classical computers encode information in bits; each bit encoding two possible values, 0 or 1.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="85548-128">一个量子位同时编码两个值：0 和 1。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-128">One qubit encodes two values simultaneously, 0 and 1.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="85548-129">两个经典位编码 4 个可能值（00、01、10、11）之一，而两个量子位则同时编码这 4 个状态的任何叠加，虽然我们在度量时只能获取这些值中的一个。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-129">Two classical bits encode one of 4 possible values, (00, 01, 10, 11) whereas two qubits encode any superposition of the 4 states simultaneously, although we can obtain only one of those values when measuring.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="85548-130">四个量子位同时编码 16 个值的任何叠加态。依此类推，编码的值的个数呈指数增长。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-130">Four qubits encode any superposition of 16 values simultaneously, and so on, exponentially.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="85548-131">100 个量子位能够编码的信息超出当今最大的计算机系统所能提供的信息。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-131">100 qubits can encode more information than is available in the largest computer systems today.</span></span>  

<span data-ttu-id="85548-132">另外，多个纠缠的量子位在协调工作时可以同时处理多个选项。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-132">Furthermore, when multiple entangled qubits act coherently, they can process multiple options simultaneously.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="85548-133">纠缠的量子位能够在很短的时间内处理信息，所需时间甚至少于最快的非量子系统。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-133">Entangled qubits can process information in a fraction of the time it would take even the fastest non-quantum systems.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="85548-134">数十年来，量子算法研究一直在利用这些量子特性，发现了许多创新性的技术，这些技术解决问题的速度远远快于经典算法。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-134">Harnessing these quantum attributes has been the pursuit of multiple decades of quantum algorithm research, and there are many innovative techniques that have been found that solve problems in a fraction of the time it takes to solve classically.</span></span>  

<span data-ttu-id="85548-135">最著名的一个量子算法是用于分解因子的“Shor 算法”，它可以更快地解决将大数字分解成两个质数这一经典又棘手的问题，给传统加密带来了巨大挑战  。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-135">One of the most famous quantum algorithms is _Shor's algorithm_ for factorization, which makes the classically intractable problem of factorization of a large number into two prime numbers fast enough to challenge traditional cryptography.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="85548-136">在更具建设性的方面，通过叠加、量子纠缠和量子位“不克隆”属性（意味着在未检测的情况下无法复制量子位），可以实现安全密码密钥分发算法  。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-136">On the more constructive side, algorithms for secure cryptographic key distribution are made possible by superposition, quantum entanglement, and the **no cloning** property of qubits, meaning the inability for qubits to be copied without detection.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="85548-137">“Grover 算法”重点介绍了一种量子算法技术，该技术在搜索非结构化数据时可以进一步加快速度。 </span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-137">_Grover's algorithm_ highlights a quantum algorithm technique that provides a quadratic speed-up for searching unstructured data.</span></span>

## <a name="quantum-hardware"></a><span data-ttu-id="85548-138">量子硬件</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-138">Quantum hardware</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="85548-139">在经典计算机中，位对应于硅电路中的电压水平。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-139">In classical computers, bits correspond to voltage levels in silicon circuits.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="85548-140">量子计算硬件可以通过许多不同的量子位物理实现来实现：捕获离子、超导、中性原子、电子自旋、光极化、拓扑量子位。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-140">Quantum computing hardware can be implemented by many different physical realizations of qubits: trapped ions, superconducting, neutral atoms, electron spin, light polarization, topological qubits.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="85548-141">量子硬件是一项新兴技术。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-141">Quantum hardware is an emergent technology.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="85548-142">量子位本身是脆弱的，在与环境交互时将变得不那么连贯。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-142">Qubits are fragile by nature and become less coherent as they interact with their environment.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="85548-143">需要平衡系统的保真度和可伸缩性。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-143">Balancing fidelity of the system with scalability is needed.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="85548-144">规模（即量子位数）越大，错误率越高。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-144">The larger the scale (that is, number of qubits), the higher the error rate.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="85548-145">Microsoft 正在开发基于拓扑量子位的量子计算机。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-145">Microsoft is developing a quantum computer based on topological qubits.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="85548-146">我们认为拓扑量子位受其环境变化的影响较小，因此降低了外部纠错的程度。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-146">We believe a topological qubit will be less impacted by changes in its environment, therefore reducing the degree of external error correction.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="85548-147">拓扑量子位具有更高的稳定性，并且不容易受到环境所影响，这意味着它们更容易伸缩，并保持更长的可靠时间。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-147">Topological qubits feature increased stability and resistance to environmental noise, which means they can more readily scale and remain reliable longer.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="85548-149">Microsoft 的量子程序的独特性在于，我们专注于扩展系统的每一个组件，为用户带来真正的量子影响力。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-149">Microsoft's quantum program is unique in that we focus on scaling each and every component of the system to deliver real quantum impact.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="85548-150">该综合性方法涉及：</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="85548-150">This comprehensive approach involves:</span></span>
